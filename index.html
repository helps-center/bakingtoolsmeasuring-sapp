1. Measuring Stuff

Dry Measuring Cups

You use these for stuff like flour, sugar, cocoa, and oats.

They usually come in a set of different sizes (1 cup, ½ cup, ⅓ cup, and ¼ cup).

Quick tip: Use a spoon to put the dry stuff in the cup, and then use something flat, like a knife, to get rid of the extra so it's level.

Liquid Measuring Cups

These are see-through and usually have a spout.

They show you how much you have in cups, milliliters, or fluid ounces.

Make sure you're eye-level with the cup when you measure to get it right.

Measuring Spoons

For small amounts like a teaspoon, tablespoon, ½ teaspoon, or ¼ teaspoon.

You need these for things like baking powder, baking soda, salt, and extracts.

Kitchen Scale

This weighs your ingredients in grams or ounces.

It's really exact, so it's good for baking, especially when you're dealing with flour.

2. Mixing Stuff

Mixing Bowls

You can get these in stainless steel, glass, or plastic.

They come in sizes from small (1 cup) to extra large (5–6 liters).

Good to know: stainless steel bowls last a long time. Glass bowls are okay for the microwave.

Whisks

Balloon whisk: good for getting air into eggs, cream, or batter.

Flat whisk: good for sauces or small amounts of stuff.

They are made of stainless steel or have a silicone coating.

Spoons &amp; Spatulas

Wooden spoons: strong and can be used for lots of stuff.

Silicone spatulas: bendy, can handle heat, and great for mixing batter and getting everything out of the bowl.

Slotted spoons: good when you need to get rid of liquid from a mixture.

Hand Mixer / Stand Mixer

Hand mixer: easy to carry around, good for small stuff.

Stand mixer: strong, you don't have to hold it, good for bread dough, cakes, and making a lot of stuff.

Things that attach to it:

Paddle/flat beater: for mixing in general.

Whisk: for whipping cream and eggs.

Dough hook: for making bread or pizza dough.

Rolling Pins

You roll these over dough to make it flat for cookies, pastries, and pie crusts.

Types:

Solid wooden: the old way, lasts a long time.

Silicone-coated: nothing sticks to it.

Adjustable: helps you roll the dough to the same thickness all over.

Pastry Blender / Cutter

You use this to mix butter into flour for pastry dough.

Makes pie crusts or biscuits flaky.

Dough Scraper / Bench Scraper

You use this to pick up, cut, or shape dough.

It also helps you clean flour off the counter.

Sifters &amp; Sieves

You use these to get air into flour and get rid of lumps.

Good for light cakes and pastries.

3. Baking Prep Stuff

Baking Pans &amp; Trays

Cake pans: can be round, square, or like a rectangle.

Loaf pans: for bread or pound cake.

Muffin/cupcake tins: usually nothing sticks to them, or they're silicone.

Baking sheets: flat for cookies, roasting nuts, or pastry.

They can be metal (aluminum, steel), silicone, or glass.

Cooling Racks

These let air flow around what you baked, so it doesn't get soggy.

Good for cookies, cakes, and breads.

Parchment Paper &amp; Silicone Mats

Nothing sticks to these when you bake.

You don't need to grease the pans as much.

Silicone mats can be used again.

Oven Thermometer

Makes sure your oven is at the right temperature.

Keeps you from baking stuff too much or not enough.

4. Special Baking Stuff

Pastry Bags &amp; Tips

You use these to squeeze out frosting, batter, or choux pastry.

The tips make different designs for writing, flowers, stars, or shells.

Cookie Cutters

Different shapes for cookies, biscuits, or fondant.

Can be metal or plastic.

Rolling Guides / Thickness Rings

Make sure your dough is the same thickness all over.

Bread Proofing Stuff

Banneton baskets: help dough rise in a certain shape.

Dough scoring knife: for making designs in bread.

Pastry Brushes

Made of silicone or natural bristles.

You use them for egg wash, butter, or glazes.

5. Other Helpful Stuff

Thermometers (instant-read, candy thermometer): Important for getting sugar stages or bread just right.

Timer / Stopwatch: Keeps you from over-baking.

Spatula Turner: For taking out cookies or delicate baked goods.

Rolling mats / pastry boards: Nothing sticks to these when you roll out dough.

Key Baking Tips

Use the right measuring tools—baking is like science, you have to be exact.

Silicone, wood, and stainless steel are good choices.

Keep your stuff organized so it's easier to find. Keep small bowls, a whisk, and a spatula where you're working.
